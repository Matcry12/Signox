## Getting Started

### Step 1: Create Your Account

1. Click **Register** in the navigation bar
2. Fill in your details:
   - First Name & Last Name
   - Username (unique identifier)
   - Email address
   - Password (choose a strong one!)
3. Click **Create Account**

### Step 2: Set Up Your Profile

After registration:
1. Go to **Profile** from the navigation
2. Upload an avatar (optional)
3. Write a short bio about yourself
4. **Important:** Select your **Skill Level**:
   - **Beginner** - New to sign language
   - **Intermediate** - Know some basics
   - **Advanced** - Looking to master complex signs

> Your skill level determines which lessons are recommended to you!

## Lessons & Categories

### Finding Lessons

**Method 1: Browse by Category**
- Go to **Topics** from the navigation
- Click on a category (e.g., "Greetings", "Numbers", "Family")
- View all lessons within that category

**Method 2: Browse All Lessons**
- Go to **Library** from the navigation
- Use filters:
  - **Category filter** - Select specific topic
  - **Difficulty filter** - Easy, Medium, or Hard
  - **Search** - Find lessons by keyword

**Method 3: Use the Book Shelf**
- On the home page, hover over books on the shelf
- Each book represents a category
- Click to view lessons in that category

### Lesson Structure

Each lesson contains:

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Video** | Demonstration video showing signs |
| **Description** | Overview of what you'll learn |
| **Content** | Detailed written explanation |
| **Vocabulary** | Individual signs with images/videos |
| **Related Lessons** | Continue learning with similar topics |

### Lesson Actions

- **Save Lesson** - Bookmark for quick access later
- **Mark Complete** - Track your progress
- **Flashcard Mode** - Practice vocabulary interactively

## Vocabulary & Flashcards

### Understanding Vocabulary Cards

Each vocabulary item includes:
- **Word** - The term in written language
- **Meaning** - The sign language equivalent
- **Description** - Additional context or usage notes
- **Media** - Image or video demonstration

### Flashcard Practice Mode

The flashcard system helps you memorize vocabulary through active recall.

**How to Use:**

1. Open any lesson
2. Click **Flashcard Mode** button
3. You'll see a card with the word on front

**Controls:**
| Action | Method |
|--------|--------|
| Flip Card | Click the card OR press **Space** |
| Next Card | Click **→** OR press **Right Arrow** |
| Previous Card | Click **←** OR press **Left Arrow** |

**Visual Progress:**
- Progress dots at the bottom show your position
- Active dot = current card
- Filled dots = reviewed cards
- Empty dots = cards ahead

**Recommended Practice:**
1. Try to recall the sign before flipping
2. Check your answer by flipping
3. Go through all cards multiple times
4. Practice daily for best retention

## Quizzes & Assessments

### Quiz Structure

Quizzes test your knowledge after completing lessons.

**Question Types:**
- **Multiple Choice** - Select one correct answer
- **True/False** - Determine if statement is correct

**Quiz Features:**
- Some quizzes have time limits
- Passing score is typically **70%**
- You can retry quizzes unlimited times

### Taking a Quiz

1. Go to **Quizzes** from navigation
2. Select a quiz (usually linked to a lesson)
3. Read each question carefully
4. Select your answer
5. Click **Submit Quiz**

### Understanding Results

After submission, you'll see:

```
┌────────────────────────────────────┐
│         YOUR SCORE: 85%            │
│       ✓ CONGRATULATIONS!           │
│                                    │
```

## Tips for Success

### Daily Practice Routine

**Recommended Schedule:**

| Time | Activity | Duration |
|------|----------|----------|
| Morning | Review flashcards | 10 min |
| Afternoon | Complete 1 lesson | 20 min |
| Evening | Take a quiz | 10 min |

### Best Practices

1. **Consistency Over Intensity**
   - 15 minutes daily > 2 hours once a week
   - Set reminders for daily practice

2. **Active Learning**
   - Don't just watch videos, practice along
   - Use flashcards for active recall
   - Test yourself regularly

3. **Spaced Repetition**
   - Review completed lessons periodically
   - Return to challenging vocabulary
   - Use saved lessons for revision

4. **Engage with Community**
   - Ask questions in the forum
   - Share your progress
   - Help others (teaching reinforces learning!)

5. **Set Goals**
   - Weekly: Complete X lessons
   - Monthly: Pass X quizzes
   - Overall: Reach Advanced level

### Difficulty Progression

| Your Level | Start With | Then Move To |
|------------|------------|--------------|
| Beginner | Easy lessons | Medium lessons |
| Intermediate | Medium lessons | Hard lessons |
| Advanced | Hard lessons | Help others! |
